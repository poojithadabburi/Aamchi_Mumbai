Sanjay Gandhi National Park

Sanjay Gandhi National Park (SGNP) is a sprawling protected area located in the Mumbai metropolitan region of Maharashtra, India. It is one of the most popular parks in the area, known for its diverse flora and fauna as well as its historical significance.

Location
Location: Borivali East, Mumbai, Maharashtra, India
Established: 1996 (formerly known as Borivali National Park)

Flora and Fauna
Biodiversity: The park is home to over 1,300 species of plants, 250 species of birds, and a variety of mammals, including:
Mammals: Leopards, spotted deer, sambar deer, and various species of monkeys.
Birds: Parrots, kingfishers, and various migratory birds.
Reptiles and Insects: Numerous species of snakes, lizards, and butterflies.

Activities
Trekking: Popular routes for trekking enthusiasts, with trails varying in difficulty.
Bird Watching: A haven for bird watchers, especially during the migratory season.
Photography: Scenic landscapes and diverse wildlife make it a great spot for photography.

Attractions
Kanheri Caves: An ancient complex of rock-cut Buddhist caves dating back to the 1st century BCE, located within the park. These caves feature intricate carvings and ancient sculptures.
Lion and Tiger Safari: A guided safari experience where visitors can see lions and tigers in a natural setting.
Nature Trails: Various walking and trekking paths for nature enthusiasts and hikers, offering opportunities to explore the park's diverse ecosystem.

Best Time to Visit
The ideal time to visit is from October to March when the weather is cooler and more pleasant for outdoor activities.

Sanjay Gandhi National Park is a remarkable blend of nature, history, and wildlife, making it a must-visit for both locals and tourists looking to escape the hustle and bustle of Mumbai.
