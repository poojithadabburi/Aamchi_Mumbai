Nehru Science Centre

Nehru Science Centre is a prominent science museum located in Worli, Mumbai. It aims to promote scientific literacy and offers a hands-on experience through various exhibits and interactive displays.

Location
Location: Worli, Mumbai, Maharashtra, India
Type: Science museum

History
Inaugurated in 1977, the Nehru Science Centre was established to honor Jawaharlal Nehru, India's first Prime Minister. It was developed as a center for science education and to inspire curiosity among visitors of all ages.

Attractions
Interactive Exhibits: The center features over 500 exhibits related to science, technology, and environmental conservation, designed to engage visitors.
Planetarium: The on-site planetarium offers shows that explore astronomy and space science, making it a popular attraction for families and students.

Features
Educational Programs: The Nehru Science Centre conducts workshops, science demonstrations, and educational programs aimed at students and the general public.
Outdoor Exhibits: The surrounding gardens include outdoor exhibits and installations that encourage exploration and interaction with scientific concepts.

Best Time to Visit
The center is open year-round, but visiting on weekdays is recommended for a quieter experience. Special events and exhibitions are often scheduled during weekends and school holidays.

Nehru Science Centre is a must-visit for anyone interested in science and technology, providing a unique opportunity to explore and learn in an engaging environment.
