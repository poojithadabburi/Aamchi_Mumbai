Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus(CSMT)

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus (CST), formerly known as Victoria Terminus, is a historic railway station in Mumbai, India. Here are the key details:

History
Construction: CST was built between 1878 and 1888, designed to commemorate Queen Victoria’s Golden Jubilee.
Renaming: It was renamed in 1996 in honor of the Maratha warrior king Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj.

Architecture
Design: The station is an outstanding example of Victorian Gothic Revival architecture, combining Indian architectural elements with Gothic styles.
Features: The building is adorned with intricate carvings, stained glass windows, and a notable clock tower, reaching a height of 65 meters (213 feet).

Location
Setting: CST is located in the bustling Fort area of South Mumbai and serves as a key hub for the Central Railways.

Facilities
Passenger Amenities: CST provides various facilities, including waiting rooms, food stalls, and ticket counters.
Connectivity: It serves both suburban and long-distance trains, making it a vital part of Mumbai's transportation network.

Surroundings
Nearby Landmarks: CST is close to other historical sites, including the Bombay High Court, CST’s iconic local markets, and the Chatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Museum.
Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus is not only a crucial transportation hub but also a stunning architectural landmark that reflects Mumbai's rich history and cultural heritage.
