Banganga Tank

Banganga Tank is a historic water tank located in the Malabar Hill area of Mumbai, known for its spiritual significance and ancient temples.

Location
Location: Banganga Tank, Walkeshwar, Malabar Hill, Mumbai, Maharashtra, India
Type: Historical site

History
Dating back to the 12th century, Banganga Tank is believed to have been created by the Hindu king Bhulabhai Deshpande. The tank is associated with the myth of Lord Rama, who is said to have visited this site during his exile.

Attractions
Temples: The tank is surrounded by several ancient temples, making it a popular pilgrimage site.
Scenic Beauty: The peaceful environment and beautiful surroundings attract visitors seeking tranquility.

Features
Cultural Significance: The site is not only a water source but also an important cultural heritage site for the local community.
Festivals: Various Hindu festivals are celebrated at the tank, drawing devotees and tourists alike.

Best Time to Visit
The best time to visit Banganga Tank is during the early morning or late evening when the atmosphere is serene and cooler.

Banganga Tank is a must-visit for those interested in history, spirituality, and culture, offering a glimpse into Mumbai's rich heritage.
