Haji Ali

Haji Ali Dargah is a renowned mosque and tomb located on an islet off the coast of Worli, Mumbai, India. It is one of the most iconic landmarks in the city, attracting both devotees and tourists.

History
Built: The dargah was built in 1431 in memory of Haji Ali Shah Bukhari, a wealthy Muslim merchant who renounced his worldly possessions and dedicated his life to Islam.
Architecture: The structure is a fine example of Indo-Islamic architecture, featuring intricate carvings and a stunning white marble fa√ßade.

Location
Location: Haji Ali Dargah, Worli Sea Face, Mumbai, Maharashtra, India
Type: Mosque and Tomb

Spiritual Significance
Pilgrimage Site: Haji Ali Dargah is a significant place of worship for Muslims, as well as for people of various faiths who come to pay their respects.
Miracles and Beliefs: It is believed that many miracles have occurred at the dargah, drawing thousands of visitors, especially during the annual Urs festival, which commemorates the saint's death.

Nearby Attraction
Worli Sea Face: A popular promenade offering stunning views of the Arabian Sea and the Mumbai skyline.
Mahalaxmi Temple: A historic Hindu temple located nearby.

Haji Ali Dargah is not only a place of worship but also a cultural and architectural gem, symbolizing Mumbai's rich diversity and heritage.
